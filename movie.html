The Tale

During World War II, Lt. Gen. Leslie Groves Jr. appoints physicist J. Robert Oppenheimer to work on the top-secret Manhattan Project. Oppenheimer and a team of scientists spend years developing and designing the atomic bomb. Their work comes to fruition on July 16, 1945, when they witness the world's first nuclear explosion, forever changing the course of history. The film explores the moral complexities, political intrigue, and the devastating consequences of creating the most destructive weapon known to mankind, as Oppenheimer grapples with his role as "the father of the atomic bomb" and the subsequent security hearings that threatened to destroy his legacy.
